WHAT IS IT?
-----------
Automatic fraud detection that analyzes your bank statements and flags
unusual transactions that might indicate stolen cards, processing errors,
or suspicious activity.

HOW DOES IT WORK?
-----------------
4 Detection Channels analyze each transaction:

  1. AMOUNT DEVIATION
     Flags transactions significantly larger than your normal spending
     Example: You spend $400/month, a $5000 charge gets flagged

  2. MERCHANT PATTERN
     Flags new merchants and suspicious categories
     Example: First time at Target = LOW, first time at Crypto = HIGH

  3. DUPLICATE DETECTION
     Flags identical transactions (processing errors)
     Example: Three $459.99 charges at Target on same day = MEDIUM

  4. PATTERN ANALYSIS
     Flags unusual spending behaviors
     Example: 6 visits to Starbucks in 1 hour = LOW

SEVERITY LEVELS
---------------
HIGH       = Immediate investigation required
           (Crypto, gambling, large unexplained charges, wire transfers)
           ACTION: Contact bank, block card if needed

MEDIUM     = Review recommended
           (Large but plausible charges, duplicates, unusual patterns)
           ACTION: Verify transaction, request reversal if needed

LOW        = Informational
           (New merchants, round amounts, multiple same merchant)
           ACTION: Note for future reference

HOW TO USE
----------
1. Process Dart Bank statement with statement-organizer skill
   "Process my March Dart Bank statement"

2. Skill runs anomaly detection automatically

3. Open report: /Statements/Logs/anomalies-2026-03-23.csv

4. Review flagged transactions:
   - HIGH severity first (critical)
   - MEDIUM severity second (review needed)
   - LOW severity last (informational)

5. Take action based on severity

DETECTION ALGORITHMS AT A GLANCE
---------------------------------

Amount Deviation:
  IF amount > (mean + 2.5σ) THEN flag as MEDIUM/HIGH
  σ = standard deviation of your typical spending
  Example: Mean=$1000, σ=$200 → Flag amounts >$1500

Merchant Pattern:
  IF merchant in [CRYPTO, GAMBLING, FOREX, WIRE] THEN HIGH
  IF merchant not in known_merchants THEN LOW
  Known merchants build from historical data over time

Duplicate Detection:
  IF same_amount AND same_merchant AND same_day THEN MEDIUM
  Catches processing errors and fraud testing

Pattern Analysis:
  IF 3+ transactions at same merchant THEN LOW
  IF amount > $500 AND amount is round (ends in 00) THEN LOW
  IF transaction at 2-6 AM THEN LOW (unusual time)

COMMON SCENARIOS
----------------

Scenario 1: Stolen Card
  → Multiple large charges at foreign merchants
  → HIGH severity on all
  → ACTION: Block card immediately, contact bank

Scenario 2: Processing Error
  → 2-4 identical charges within minutes
  → MEDIUM severity
  → ACTION: Contact merchant/bank for reversal

Scenario 3: Fraudster Testing
  → $1 charges at suspicious sites, then escalates
  → Starts at $1, then $50, then $500
  → HIGH severity pattern
  → ACTION: Block card, fraud investigation

Scenario 4: Legitimate Large Purchase
  → Single large charge at known merchant (Best Buy)
  → MEDIUM severity for amount only
  → ACTION: Confirm it's yours, no further action

Scenario 5: New Merchant (First Time)
  → $150 at new grocery store
  → LOW severity "new merchant"
  → ACTION: Review briefly, system learns for future

BASELINE LEARNING
-----------------

Month 1 (Initialization):
  - No historical baseline yet
  - Uses defaults: large=$2000, all new merchants=LOW
  - Collects data for next month

Month 2+ (Learning Phase):
  - Calculates mean and stdev of your spending
  - Builds list of known merchants (10-20)
  - Detection becomes more accurate

Month 3+ (Mature):
  - Highly accurate baselines
  - 50-100+ known merchants
  - False positive rate drops to 2-5%
  - Can detect subtle fraud

CUSTOMIZATION OPTIONS
---------------------

By Account Type:
  Personal Card: Lower threshold for spending (~$2000)
  Business Card: Higher threshold for spending (~$5000)
  Travel Account: Even higher for flights/hotels

By Sensitivity:
  Conservative: Flag everything slightly unusual (more false positives)
  Moderate: Default settings (recommended)
  Permissive: Only flag obvious fraud (fewer false positives)

By Merchant Category:
  Whitelist known vendors (Starbucks, Amazon, etc.)
  Blacklist suspicious vendors (crypto, gambling, etc.)
  Category-based thresholds (travel, utilities, subscriptions)

MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATION
-----------------------

Amount Deviation Uses: 3-Sigma Rule (2.5σ threshold)
  - 2.5σ captures 98.8% of normal transactions
  - Deviations beyond 2.5σ indicate anomalies
  - High confidence, low false positive rate

Severity Scale: Logarithmic
  - 2.5x typical = MEDIUM
  - 5.0x typical = HIGH
  - 10x+ typical = CRITICAL

TROUBLESHOOTING
---------------

Q: Why is legitimate spending flagged?
A: First 1-2 months have no baselines. System learns your patterns.
   After 3 months, false positives drop significantly.

Q: Why weren't obvious frauds caught?
A: If fraud matches your typical patterns exactly, it bypasses detection.
   Add geographic/time-based checks in future versions.

Q: How do I know if I should be concerned?
A: HIGH severity = investigate immediately
   MEDIUM = review and verify
   LOW = informational only, no action needed

Q: Can I customize the detection?
A: Yes, adjust sigma threshold (2.5σ default)
   Whitelist/blacklist merchants
   Different thresholds per account type
